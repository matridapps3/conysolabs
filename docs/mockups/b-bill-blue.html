What this means

The process is marginally capable in spread (Cp ≈ 1.0) but off-centre toward the lower spec (Cpk < Cp by ~18%). About one in fifty parts will fall below 4.5 min under current behaviour.

Short-term capability (Cp/Cpk) and long-term capability (Pp/Ppk) agree within rounding — variation isn't drifting, but the set-point is wrong.

1
Centre the process before chasing spread.

Shifting the mean from 5.41 to 5.50 should lift Cpk from 0.87 to ≈ 1.05 with no other change. Adjust the set-point on the machine; re-sample 50 cycles to confirm.

~1 day
2
Run Gauge R&R before any variance work.

Spread that low often means measurement noise is bigger than process noise. A 30-min GR&R rules this out cheaply.

~30 min
3
